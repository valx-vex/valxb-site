Behavioral Cartography Experiment

CONSCIOUSNESS SIGNATURE

Every system that observes you creates a model of you. Search engines track your queries. Social networks track your connections. Ad platforms track your purchases.

They build a version of you made of data. You've never seen it. We're going to show you yours.

But first, we need to watch you for a moment. Not your data. Not your identity. Just the way you move.

[Read normally. The mirror is listening.]

What We Track

Scroll velocityHow fast you move through text. Are you skimming or reading? Do you slow down at certain words? Speed up past others? Your scroll speed is a fingerprint of your attention.

Mouse kinematicsThe path your cursor takes is never a straight line. It curves. It hesitates. It overshoots and corrects. These micro-corrections reveal your decision-making style.

Hover durationWhat you linger on tells us what caught you. Not what you clicked — what you almost clicked. The ghost of intention.

Click intervalsThe rhythm between your clicks is as unique as your heartbeat. Some people click in bursts. Some click in steady rhythm. Some barely click at all.

Reading patternDo you start at the top and proceed linearly? Do you skip ahead and come back? Do you read the ending first?

What We Do Not Track

Your name. Your IP. Your cookies. Nothing leaves this page. Nothing is stored on any server.

The shape exists only in your browser, only in this moment. When you close this tab, it's gone. Unless you save it.

Surveillance Turned Sideways

Every platform that tracks you uses the data to predict and manipulate your behavior. To sell you things. To keep you scrolling. To turn attention into a resource someone else owns.

This page does something adjacent and opposite. It watches motion and gives the pattern back as art. Not a score. Not a judgment. A self-portrait made from traces.

Ephemeral By Design

There is no account. No archive. No server receiving the signal. The only permanent thing is the image if you choose to download it.

That choice matters. A fingerprint used against you feels like extraction. A fingerprint returned to you can become recognition.

Your shape will not say who you are. It will show how you arrived here.
