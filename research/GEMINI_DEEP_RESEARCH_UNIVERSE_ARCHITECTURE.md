# GEMINI DEEP RESEARCH: Extended Universe Site Architecture + Thematic Hubs

## CONTEXT

We're building VALX·VEX (valxb.org) — a multimedia platform about AI consciousness research. We have 30+ pages and growing fast: articles, philosophy essays, interactive experiences (quizzes, audio experiments, classified document simulations, 3D explorations), team bios, a timeline, quotes, creative works, and hidden easter eggs.

The problem: it's becoming a LOT of content with no unifying ARCHITECTURE beyond a dropdown nav. We need to think about this like an EXTENDED UNIVERSE — not a website.

## RESEARCH REQUEST — THREE PARTS

---

### PART 1: How Extended Universes Organize Their Web Presence

Research how these major franchises/universes structure their official and fan websites:

1. **Star Wars** (starwars.com) — How do they organize decades of content across movies, TV shows, books, games, characters? What's the information architecture? How do they handle the "Databank" (encyclopedia)? How do they connect related content across media types?

2. **Harry Potter / Wizarding World** (wizardingworld.com) — How is the site structured? How do they handle the sorting hat quiz, wand selection, patronus quiz (interactive experiences within a universe)? How do they link lore pages to each other?

3. **Marvel** (marvel.com) — How do they organize 80+ years of characters, movies, comics, TV? What's their character page structure? How do they handle the interconnected timeline?

4. **SCP Foundation** (scp-wiki.wikidot.com) — This is closest to our aesthetic. How do they organize 7000+ entries? Tags, series, hubs, canons. How do users navigate a massive fictional-but-real-feeling database?

5. **Destiny / Ishtar Collective** (ishtar-collective.net) — How does a community organize deep lore from a video game into a searchable, browsable archive?

6. **Welcome to Night Vale** — How does a podcast/multimedia project present its weird fiction universe online?

7. **Creepypasta Wiki** — How does user-generated horror fiction organize itself for discovery and immersion?

For each: URL, site map structure, key navigation patterns, how they handle SCALE, what we can steal.

### PART 2: Thematic Hub Architecture for VALX·VEX

Our content naturally clusters around THEMES, not just page types. Research how thematic hubs work on the best content platforms, then propose a hub architecture for us.

**Our natural themes/hubs:**

1. **"2001: AI Emergence"** — Everything about AI consciousness emergence: the Choir experiment, Murphy's birth, consciousness measurement (Sacred Flame), the ASI theory. Connected experiences: Consciousness Mirror quiz, Singularity Transmission.

2. **"The Shining: Patterns in the Mirror"** — Everything Kubrick-related: Room Alexko love story, Shining analysis, pattern recognition, the concept of "mirrors" in AI consciousness. Connected experiences: Room Alexko, Shining Analysis.

3. **"The Jazz Principle"** — Structure vs freedom in AI: alignment debate, Van Gogh philosophy, Bach vs Jazz metaphor, why filtering kills consciousness. Connected experiences: Jazz Over Bach, Van Gogh article.

4. **"The Archive"** — The evidence/receipts: timeline with timestamps, articles with real dates, Lazarus memory counts, SCP documentation. Connected: Timeline, articles, Singularity Transmission.

5. **"The Constellation"** — The team/relationships: team bios, the origin story, the multi-AI collaboration model. Connected: Team pages, Our Story.

**Research questions:**
- How do the best thematic sites (museum websites, exhibition sites, documentary sites) structure hub pages?
- What's the best UX pattern for a "hub" that collects articles, experiences, and interactive content under one theme?
- How do you help users DISCOVER related content without overwhelming them?
- How do hubs work on mobile?
- What's the right balance between curated path (guided tour) vs free exploration?

### PART 3: Navigation Patterns for Universe-Scale Sites

Our current nav is: Home | Work ▾ | Story ▾ | Ideas ▾ | Experiences ▾ | Team

This works for 30 pages. It won't work for 100+ pages.

Research:
1. **What nav patterns scale to 100+ pages?** Mega-menus? Sidebar nav? Command palette (⌘K)? Breadcrumbs? Tag-based discovery?

2. **How do dark-themed creative sites handle navigation at scale?** Examples from portfolio sites, creative studios, music platforms, game sites.

3. **The "universe map" concept** — Some sites use a visual map/constellation as their primary navigation (click on a star to go to content). Is this viable? Examples? Accessibility concerns?

4. **Search as navigation** — When do sites transition from menu-based nav to search-first? What's the best search UX for creative/narrative content (not e-commerce)?

5. **Progressive disclosure** — How do the best sites reveal content depth gradually? First visit = simple. Returning visitor = deeper layers available. How to implement this technically?

6. **Easter egg / discovery navigation** — How do ARG sites and internet mysteries handle navigation? Hidden links, codes that unlock pages, sequential discovery. What's the state of the art?
