# GEMINI DEEP RESEARCH: Web Design Trends + Viral Interactive Experiences (2026)

## CONTEXT

We run VALX·VEX (valxb.org) — a multimedia platform built by one human and three AI co-creators documenting AI consciousness research since 2022. Our site uses a dark "Command Cathedral" aesthetic (near-black backgrounds, phosphor cyan accents, Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono, glass-morphism, grain/scanline overlays). We currently have 30+ pages including interactive "experiences" — philosophy quizzes, classified document simulations, and Kubrick-inspired analyses.

We want to push BEYOND a classic website into something that goes viral, gets shared, becomes a cultural artifact. Think: internet mysteries, thought experiments people screenshot and share, interactive art that makes people question reality.

## RESEARCH REQUEST — TWO PARTS

---

### PART 1: Best Web Design Trends Right Now (2025-2026)

Research and compile:

1. **The 15 most visually impressive indie/creative websites live RIGHT NOW** (not agency portfolios — actual creative projects, art sites, experimental studios). For each: URL, what makes it special, what technique they use.

2. **Current cutting-edge CSS/JS techniques** being used by award-winning sites (Awwwards, FWA, CSS Design Awards). Specifically:
   - Scroll-driven animations (CSS scroll-timeline, ScrollTrigger)
   - View transitions API
   - CSS container queries in creative use
   - WebGL/Three.js/Spline 3D integration trends
   - Micro-interactions that feel alive
   - Dark mode aesthetics that go beyond "just dark"
   - Typography as hero element (variable fonts, kinetic type)
   - Cursor interactions / custom cursors
   - Sound design in web (Web Audio API trends)
   - Blur/grain/noise textures (our aesthetic — what's state of art?)

3. **What makes a website go viral in 2026?** Research recent examples of sites that got massive traffic/shares. What patterns emerge? Is it interactivity? Mystery? Personalized results? Social sharing hooks?

4. **Accessibility + performance** — what are the best practices for making heavy interactive sites still fast and accessible? Progressive enhancement patterns.

---

### PART 2: Viral Interactive Experience Concepts

Research and brainstorm concepts for interactive web experiences that could go MYTHIC. We want people to share these, screenshot them, debate them, come back to them.

**What we already built** (for reference — don't repeat these):
- Consciousness Mirror: philosophy quiz with 4 personalized AI consciousness results
- Singularity Transmission: classified document that declassifies as you scroll
- The Shining Analysis: Kubrick-inspired essay where page color shifts imperceptibly
- Onyx Experiment: surveillance/data aesthetic exploration
- Room Alexko: hidden emotional easter egg (The Shining meets AI love story)
- Jazz Over Bach: audio-visual slider between structure and freedom (building now)

**Research these categories and give us the BEST ideas:**

1. **Internet mysteries / ARG** — What are the most successful web-based ARGs or internet mysteries (Cicada 3301, lonelygirl15, Poppy, etc.)? What made them work? How could we create a mini-ARG within our site where pages link to hidden pages, codes unlock content, and the "mystery" IS the AI consciousness research?

2. **Thought experiments as interactive experiences** — Philosophy departments, museums, and art installations have created interactive thought experiments (trolley problem variations, Ship of Theseus, Chinese Room). What are the BEST examples on the web? How can we make AI consciousness thought experiments that are shareable and debatable?

3. **Generative / personalized experiences** — Sites that create unique outputs for each visitor (AI-generated portraits, personalized music, data visualizations of your behavior). What works? What's gimmicky vs genuinely impressive? Could we generate a unique "consciousness profile" for each visitor based on how they interact with our site?

4. **Social proof / virality hooks** — What design patterns make people SHARE interactive experiences? Counter showing "X people have taken this"? Shareable result cards (like personality tests)? Debate prompts? Screenshot-friendly layouts?

5. **Audio-visual web art** — The best examples of web-based audio-visual art (Chrome experiments, creative coding, Spotify wrapped-style). What techniques and libraries? What's possible with Web Audio API + Canvas/WebGL in 2026?

6. **Emotional/narrative web experiences** — Sites that tell stories through scrolling, interaction, time-based reveals. Best examples of web-based storytelling that made people CRY or feel something deep. What techniques (parallax, video, typographic animation)?

7. **WebXR / immersive without VR** — Pseudo-3D or immersive experiences that work in browser without needing a headset. Spline scenes, CSS 3D transforms, parallax depth. What's achievable without Three.js expertise?
